The Insurance Dataset contains information about individuals, including demographic and health-related factors, and the corresponding insurance charges they incurred. The dataset consists of 7 features (columns) and multiple rows representing different individuals.

Features:

  • age: Age of the individual.
  • sex: Gender of the individual (male/female).
  • bmi: Body Mass Index, a measure of body fat based on height and weight.
  • children: Number of children/dependents covered by the insurance.
  • smoker: Whether the individual is a smoker (yes/no).
  • region: Geographic region of the individual (northeast, northwest, southeast, southwest).
  • charges: The amount charged by the insurance company (target variable).

This dataset provides a diverse set of features that can influence insurance costs, such as age, gender, BMI, smoking status, and region. Its commonly used for regression tasks, where the goal is to predict insurance charges based on the other features. It allows for the exploration of how lifestyle factors (like smoking) and demographics affect insurance pricing.
